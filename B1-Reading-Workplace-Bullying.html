Analyzing a Real-World Example

Read the case study below. Click on each section to reveal the story, and click on the highlighted words to see their definitions.

A senior accountant at a Sydney-based financial services firm has been awarded significant compensation after a tribunal found the company failed to protect her from workplace harassment.

Maria Rossi, who had worked at FinCorp Advisory for over ten years, filed a formal grievance last year. Her allegation was against a department manager who, she claimed, created a hostile work environment. According to Rossi, the manager constantly criticized her work in front of colleagues, excluded her from important meetings, and often gave her impossible deadlines.

Rossi initially tried to resolve the issue informally. However, when the behavior continued, she followed the company‚Äôs official procedure. The company started an internal investigation, but the process was slow and lacked clear communication. They suggested mediation, but the manager refused to participate.

Feeling she had no other choice, Rossi took her case to the Fair Work Commission. The tribunal heard evidence that other employees had witnessed the manager's behavior. They concluded that the manager's actions constituted bullying and that FinCorp Advisory was liable because it did not take reasonable steps to stop it. The manager was dismissed for serious misconduct, and the company was ordered to pay Rossi $85,000 in compensation for lost wages and emotional distress.

Communicating with Precision

When we discuss a grievance or allegation, the verbs we use are critical. They carry specific legal and professional weight. For example, "claimed" suggests a statement is unproven, while "admitted" implies accepting responsibility.

Practice using these precise reporting verbs before we move on to the discussion.

1. The company to Maria: "We think mediation is a good idea." (suggested)

The company suggested (that) Maria/they try mediation.

2. The manager: "I did not exclude her from meetings." (denied)

The manager denied excluding her from meetings. / The manager denied that he had excluded her from meetings.

3. Maria to the company: "You did not protect me." (accused)

Maria accused the company of not protecting her.
