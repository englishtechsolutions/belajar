3 Reading Passage

Instructions: Read the text. While reading, try to find one surprising fact.

Gen Z and Accounting Careers

Many young people today, called Gen Z (born between 1997 and 2012), are starting to choose jobs in accounting. In the past, accounting was often seen as boring. A 2022 study even said it was the second-most “boring” job, after data analysis. People imagined accountants sitting in quiet offices, using calculators all day. For many millennials, accounting did not look exciting.

But now, things are changing. Millions of baby boomers, who are older workers, are retiring from accounting. In the last five years, about 340,000 accountants have already left the profession. This creates a big gap in the workforce. Companies and organizations need new accountants, and Gen Z is beginning to fill this space.

Some students are learning accounting before they finish college. One example is the IRS Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) program. This program helps low-income people file their taxes. It started more than 50 years ago at California State University, Northridge. In 2024, hundreds of students at the university helped more than 9,000 Americans. These taxpayers received nearly $11 million in tax refunds and $3.6 million in tax credits. Through this program, students gain real-world experience and see how accounting can make a difference in people’s lives.

Teachers say that once students try accounting, their ideas about the job change. Rafael Efrat, the director of the VITA program, explained that accounting may look boring at first, but when young people practice it, they see how useful and important it is. Helping families with taxes shows that accounting is not only about numbers—it is also about supporting communities.

Besides helping others, accounting is attractive because of the salary. According to Glassdoor, accountants earn about $93,000 per year on average. With more experience, they can earn up to $122,000. Certified Public Accountants (CPAs) make even more, with salaries close to $200,000 a year. For many Gen Z students, this is a strong reason to choose the profession.

Accounting also offers stability. In a world where many jobs change quickly, accounting remains necessary. Every company, big or small, needs accountants to manage money, prepare reports, and follow tax rules. This makes accounting a career with long-term opportunities.

So, while accounting may look boring at first, many Gen Z students now see it as a smart career. It offers good pay, real-world impact, and a chance to help people. For young people who want both financial success and meaningful work, accounting is becoming an attractive choice.

9 Role Play: The Skeptical Parent

Person A (The Student)

You want to study accounting. You are excited about the salary ($93k) and the stability. You also want to join the VITA program to help people.

Person B (The Parent)

You are worried. You think accounting is too boring and your child will be unhappy. You want them to be something "exciting" like a doctor or architect.
